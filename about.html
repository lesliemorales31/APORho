About Us

Alpha Phi Omega was founded in 1925 at Lafayette College in Easton, Pennsylvania by Frank Reed Horton. It is the largest collegiate fraternity in the United States, with chapters at over 350 campuses, an active membership of over 25,000 students, and over 400,000 alumni members.

Be a Leader.

The Fraternity helps siblings become better leaders by developing their own personal strengths through service as officers, exec members, or committee members.

Be a Friend.

Alpha Phi Omega seeks to develop friendship through shared experiences, such as fraternal traditions, weekly socials, and annual events such as Pledge Mountain and Beach, semi-formal, and Pledge Retreat.

Be of Service.

The chapter service program provides opportunities to develop social awareness, friendships and leadership skills. The Fraternity completes service to the campus, community, nation, and fraternity through partnership with over 15 local organizations.

Fall 2020 Philanthropy Awards

Benevolence Farm

Benevolence Farm seeks to cultivate leadership, promote sustainable livelihoods, and reap structural change with individuals impacted by the criminal justice system in North Carolina.

Hope Renovations

Hope Renovations inspires hope in women and older adults by empowering women to pursue living-wage jobs in the construction trades via a hands-on training program, which provides repairs and renovations that enable older adults to stay in their homes as they age.

Ronald McDonald House

The House is here to care for families in life-altering moments, days and months that children spend in area hospitals.

World Relief Durham

World Relief caseworkers provide extensive case management to help refugees build stable, flourishing lives in the United States.
